## 1. Consumer Protection Law 2023 — Major Changes

This is a **comprehensive overhaul** of the 2010 law. Key new obligations:

### Platform Liability (Art. 37-39)
- Platform must **verify seller identity** (operator business registration, tax code)
- Must **remove violating listings** when notified
- Must **cooperate with authorities** on consumer protection investigations
- **Platform jointly liable** for operator conduct in certain circumstances
- Must maintain operator information for authority access

### Right to Cancel Remote Contracts (Art. 29)
- Consumers have right to cancel contracts concluded remotely/electronically within **3 working days** of receiving goods/services
- **UNLESS** the goods/services are: perishable, personalized, digital content already accessed, or **services already fully performed**

**Bus ticket question**: does departure = "service already performed"?
- Pre-departure: cancellation right likely applies (service not yet performed)
- Post-departure: right expired (service performed)
- **Legal counsel needed** on exact boundary

### Unfair Contract Terms (Art. 25-26)
Expanded list of prohibited terms in standard form contracts (ToS):
- Unilateral right to change contract terms without consumer consent
- Excessive limitation of platform liability
- Mandatory arbitration without consumer agreement
- Terms that are "obviously unfair" to the consumer

### Complaint Handling
| Step | Deadline |
|------|----------|
| Acknowledge receipt | **3 working days** |
| Resolve (simple cases) | **7 working days** |
| Resolve (complex cases) | **30 working days** |

Must have designated complaint channel accessible from the platform.

### Information Disclosure
- Clear, complete, accurate information about services before transaction
- All mandatory seller information prominently displayed
- **Confirmation step** required before payment commitment

## 2. Price Transparency

| Requirement | Source |
|-------------|--------|
| Total price shown upfront (including all fees/taxes) | CPL 2023 + Pricing Law 2023 |
| No hidden fees added at checkout | CPL 2023 |
| If booking fee separate from ticket price → disclose before payment | Decree 85/2021 |
| VAT-inclusive pricing (standard in VN) | Tax law |
| Prices in VND only for Vietnamese consumers | Currency law |
| Comparative pricing ("was X, now Y") must be truthful | Advertising Law 2012 |

## 3. Digital Contract Validity

E-Transactions Law 2023 (effective Jul 2024):
- Online booking = valid electronic contract
- Electronic messages (booking confirmations, e-tickets) have legal validity equal to paper
- Electronic signatures legally recognized
- Consumer must have opportunity to **review and confirm** before completing transaction

Booking confirmation must include: departure details, price, operator info, cancellation terms.

## 4. Advertising Regulations

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| Truthful claims | All advertising must be accurate and verifiable |
| Superlative claims | "Best", "cheapest", "fastest" need evidence |
| Comparative advertising | Cannot falsely compare with competitors |
| Digital advertising | Must be clearly identifiable as advertising |
| Social media / influencer | Must disclose sponsorship |
| Penalties | **30-70 million VND** for organizations |

## 5. Promotions & Discounts

| Rule | Detail |
|------|--------|
| MOIT registration required | Discount >50% OR total promotion value >100M VND |
| Max promotion period | **90 days** per program (extension possible) |
| Discount cap | Generally **50% max** (higher needs approval) |
| Lucky draw / contest | Must register **regardless of value** |
| Records retention | **3 years** |
| Display requirements | Original price, discounted price, conditions, period |

Operator discounts on ticket prices: if operator offers >50% off → check MOIT registration requirement.

Platform-sponsored promotions (first-booking discount, referral): register with MOIT if total value >100M VND.

## Compliance Actions

| Action | Urgency | Detail |
|--------|---------|--------|
| Legal review of ToS | Before launch | Against 2023 unfair terms list (Art. 25-26) |
| Refund/cancellation policy | Before launch | Comply with 3-day right. Legal opinion on bus ticket exception |
| Complaint handling system | Before launch | Acknowledge 3 days, resolve 7-30 days |
| Price transparency audit | Before launch | Total price before payment commitment |
| Review and confirm step | Before launch | Required by E-Transactions Law before payment |
| Operator verification process | Before launch | Business registration, tax code, transport license |
| Promotion registration process | Before marketing campaigns | For campaigns >100M VND or >50% discount |
| Advertising compliance review | Before ad campaigns | No unsubstantiated superlative claims |

## Open Risks

1. **3-day cancellation right** could significantly impact no-refund bus ticket policies — major industry disruption
2. Platform liability for operator failures (delayed/cancelled trips) is broad under 2023 law
3. Class action provisions enable consumer groups to sue platforms — new exposure
4. Promotion registration creates compliance burden for frequent marketing campaigns
5. E-Commerce Law 2025 (Jul 2026) adds: 24-hour complaint response, enhanced content removal obligations
