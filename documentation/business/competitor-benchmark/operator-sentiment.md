# Operator Sentiment & Churn Drivers

> Operator research data. For BB's product differentiators that address these pain points, see [`market-research/competitive-advantages.md`](../market-research/competitive-advantages.md).

> Structural analysis of operator motivations. No documented public departures found -- findings are inference from platform structure and revealed preferences. Research date: 2026-06-17.

### What Operators Value Most (by revealed preference)

1. **Brand control** (HIGHEST) -- FUTA (30M pax/year) and Thanh Buoi invested in proprietary apps. Thanh Buoi app (com.vexere.thanhbuoi) ironically built on VeXeRe infra -- shows depth of dependency.
2. **Settlement speed / cash flow** -- VeXeRe advertises "direct bank account deposits" as BMS selling point, implying operators suffered delays.
3. **Commission rate** -- Operators accept 8-12% given VeXeRe reach (700+ list). Threshold ~15% -- above triggers push to direct channels.
4. **Tech support / BMS capabilities** -- VeXeRe BMS (GPS, driver apps, cargo, e-invoicing, white-label) is genuine operational value small operators can't replicate.

### VeXeRe BMS Lock-In

- **Two-layer lock-in:** distribution partners AND software customers. Exiting distribution = losing software stack.
- **Data portability not guaranteed.** Custom features need "separate negotiation and fees." No published exit terms.
- **White-label apps run on VeXeRe infrastructure.** Operator exits the platform, app goes with it.
- **Mitigation:** large operators (FUTA) built independent stacks specifically to avoid this dependency.

### Multi-Platform Listing Behavior

Multi-homing is the norm. FUTA lists on own platform + VeXeRe + redBus + Baolau + 12Go + Omio simultaneously. Mid-tier operators typically list on VeXeRe + 1-2 tourist OTAs. This is rational: each OTA serves different demographics (domestic commuters vs international tourists vs mobile-first younger riders).

### Key Churn Triggers (inferred)

1. **Commission rate increases** -- any upward revision triggers cost-benefit recalculation against direct channels
2. **Platform pricing conflicting with operator strategy** -- OTA flash sales or dynamic pricing that undercuts the operator's own advertised price
3. **Settlement disputes or cash flow squeezes** -- delayed or disputed payouts erode trust faster than high commission
4. **Desire to own customer loyalty** -- repeat bookings going through the OTA mean the operator never builds a direct relationship
5. **OTA onboarding competing operator on same routes with active promotion** -- perceived favoritism or subsidized competition
6. **Brand dilution reaching threshold** -- operator identity disappears behind the OTA brand

### Regulatory Context

VCC (Vietnam Competition Commission) tightening oversight of OTA platforms. Dec 2024 Tuoi Tre News reported foreign OTAs "gaining control of Vietnam's online market." Regulatory pressure applies to all OTAs for transparency in commission structures, settlement terms, and data practices.

### BB's Strategic Opportunity

Position directly against lock-in and opacity:

- **Publish commission rates transparently.** No competitor does this.
- **Guarantee data portability.** Operators own their customer data.
- **T+1 settlement as headline differentiator.** Fastest published terms in market.
- **"Shopify for bus operators"** directly addresses the #1 concern (brand control) -- operators use BB infrastructure but maintain their own brand identity and customer relationship.
