# /creator-audience-build — Audience as Distribution Moat

## Why you'd care

The 11th cold launch with 12 signups demoralizes more than 11 months of audience compounding, and the indie founder who skipped audience build is stuck paying 3–10× warm CPA on every channel they touch. Twelve months of weekly content into a real waitlist is the difference between launching with momentum and launching into silence.

Invoke as `/creator-audience-build`. Run ≥ 90 days before launch for indie/creator products. Re-run quarterly to recalibrate channel mix.

## Pre-flight
1. Read `docs/classify/<project>.md`.
   - XL → SKIP (large companies use PR + paid + sales, not founder-as-creator)
2. Read `docs/gtm/positioning-statement-<project>.md`, `docs/gtm/buyer-persona-<project>.md`, `docs/gtm/messaging-pillars-<project>.md` if present.
3. Read `docs/gtm/launch-channel-plan-<project>.md` — audience build complements paid + community plans.
4. Audit current handles: list every public surface (Twitter/X, LinkedIn, Substack, YouTube, TikTok, GitHub, personal site), follower count, last-post recency.

## Inputs
- Founder's time per week available for content (be honest — 5h, 10h, 20h?).
- Founder's natural medium (writing? talking on camera? threads? long-form essays?).
- Audience target: B2B SaaS buyer? Indie dev? Designer? Consumer? Niche enthusiast?
- Existing audience size per channel (start-from-zero is fine; document it).
- Product launch horizon (T-90, T-180, T-365).
- Newsletter platform pref (Substack, Beehiiv, Ghost, Kit/ConvertKit, Mailchimp).
- Build-in-public comfort level (full transparency MRR? milestone-only? private until launch?).

## Process
1. **Channel pick (don't do all)**:
   - Pick **1 primary** + **1 amplifier**. Two channels well-fed beats five starved.
   - Audience × medium fit:
     - B2B SaaS buyers → LinkedIn (primary) + newsletter (Beehiiv/Substack) (amplifier).
     - Indie devs → Twitter/X (primary) + newsletter (amplifier).
     - Consumer + visual → TikTok or YouTube Shorts (primary) + Instagram (amplifier).
     - Long-form thinkers → newsletter (primary) + LinkedIn or Twitter (amplifier).
   - Comparators by lane: **Lenny Rachitsky** (Lenny's Newsletter — B2B PM/Substack), **Justin Welsh** (LinkedIn solopreneur), **Pieter Levels** (Twitter/X build-in-public), **Sahil Bloom** (Twitter→newsletter), **Ali Abdaal** (YouTube→newsletter), **Packy McCormick** (Not Boring/Substack), **Polina Pompliano** (The Profile/Substack).
2. **Newsletter platform pick**:
   - **Substack**: best discovery (recommendations + Notes), revenue split 10%+Stripe, ideal for thought leaders. Weak forms/automation.
   - **Beehiiv**: best growth tools (referral program, boost network, Magic Link forms), flat pricing. Ideal for indie hackers chasing scale.
   - **Ghost**: self-hosted or Pro; max ownership + customization; ideal if you'll outgrow $$ tiers and want owned tech.
   - **Kit (formerly ConvertKit)**: best automations + tagging; ideal for creators selling courses/products to subscribers.
   - **Mailchimp**: avoid for content; built for transactional/retail email.
   - Migrate cost is real — pick once. Beehiiv is the modal indie choice in 2025.
3. **Cadence design** (the failure mode is over-promising):
   - Newsletter: weekly is the discoverability sweet spot for first 12 months. Bi-weekly once over 5k subs is fine. Monthly = invisible.
   - Twitter/X: 1–3 posts/day if primary; long-form thread 1×/week.
   - LinkedIn: 1 post/day if primary (LinkedIn rewards daily); 3×/week if amplifier.
   - YouTube long-form: 1 video/week or 2/month; below that loses algo.
   - Shorts/TikTok: 3–7/week minimum to feed algo.
   - **Pick a cadence you can hold for 12 months, not 12 weeks.** Skipping a week on a weekly newsletter is a 5–10% unsub event.
4. **Content pillars** (3–4 themes you'll rotate):
   - Pillar 1 — **Tactics**: how-to / playbook / framework (highest save + share).
   - Pillar 2 — **Build-in-public**: progress, numbers, failures (highest follow conversion).
   - Pillar 3 — **POV/take**: opinion on industry/category (highest engagement + controversy).
   - Pillar 4 — **Behind-the-scenes**: workflow, tool stack, day-in-life (humanizes).
   - Each post should be 1 of the 4 — never "general musings".
5. **Build-in-public lever** — high-leverage for indie/SaaS:
   - Public MRR / DAU / waitlist count posts get 5–10× engagement vs feature posts.
   - Trade-off: transparency may help competitors. Most indie products under $500k ARR benefit more than they lose.
   - Pattern: monthly recap thread/post with revenue chart, top wins, top failures, next month bet.
   - Decision: full public MRR? milestone-only? private? — document.
6. **Waitlist + launchlist mechanics**:
   - Capture from day 0 — every piece of content links to waitlist landing page.
   - Landing page basics: headline + 1-line value + email field + social proof + FAQ. Keep <600 px of content above fold.
   - Tools: SavvyCal/Tally/Typeform for form; Beehiiv/Substack/Kit for storage; Senja or Testimonial.to for social proof.
   - **Referral mechanic** (compounds): "share with 3 friends to skip the line". Tools: SparkLoop (newsletter), Beehiiv built-in, Viral Loops, Maitre.
   - Conversion baselines: cold landing page → email = 1–5%. Audience post → email = 3–10%. Newsletter cross-promo → email = 5–25%.
   - Target waitlist size at launch: 1,000+ for $10/mo SaaS to expect 100–300 paying (10–30% conversion typical for warm waitlists, lower for cold).
7. **Audience → paid conversion path**:
   - Don't sell on every post — 80/20 rule (80% value, 20% offer).
   - Soft sequence: free content → newsletter → free product/lead magnet → trial → paid.
   - Direct sequence (indie SaaS): build-in-public → waitlist → launch day discount → annual plan.
   - Lifetime deal (LTD) — controversial; covers runway but caps LTV. Use only if cash crunch and product is mature enough that abuse risk is low. AppSumo channel = LTD plus negative-margin acquisition often.
   - **The most predictive metric is reply rate, not open rate.** Replies = real audience.
8. **Distribution-as-moat thesis**:
   - Product moats decay (anyone can ship a CRUD app). Distribution moats compound (audience grows interest).
   - 1k true fans @ $100/yr = $100k/yr — Kevin Kelly math.
   - Switching cost of audience to competitor ≈ 0; switching cost of competitor to *clone* your audience > infinity.
   - Implication: time spent on content compounds; time spent on features depreciates.
   - Counter: only if founder ≠ wantrepreneur. Audience build is 12+ month commitment with no early validation signal beyond followers (a vanity metric).
9. **Growth loops**:
   - **Newsletter recommendations** (Substack/Beehiiv): biggest single growth lever; turn it on day 1. 10–30% of new subs typically come from recos at scale.
   - **Cross-promo swaps**: trade newsletter mentions with creators in adjacent niches; 100–1000 subs per swap at peer-level size.
   - **Twitter→Newsletter funnel**: pin tweet links to newsletter; bio CTA; "the full version is in the newsletter".
   - **SEO long-tail**: publish 1–2 evergreen pillar posts/quarter that target long-tail keywords; compounds 6–12 months out.
   - **Podcast guesting**: each ep with a CTA to your free thing drives 50–500 subs typical; aim for 1/month.
10. **Measurement**:
    - **Vanity** (track but don't optimize): followers, total subs, impressions.
    - **Operational**: weekly net new subs, reply rate, share rate, paid conversion.
    - **Strategic**: waitlist→trial conversion, trial→paid, audience LTV, hours/week sustainable.
    - Set a **single north star** for first 6 months — usually "newsletter subscribers" or "qualified waitlist size".
11. **Burnout / pivot triggers**:
    - 4 weeks straight of skipped cadence → revisit channel pick or cadence.
    - Engagement declining 3 months straight → content pillar refresh.
    - Audience size flat 6 months → growth loop audit (recommendations, swaps, SEO).
    - Founder dread before each post → wrong medium; switch (writer trying to be on camera = miserable).

## Distribution-as-moat thesis
- 1,000 true fans @ <$X> = <$Y> ARR
- Audience switching cost to competitor: ~0 for follower, ∞ for the trust
- Time on content compounds; time on features depreciates
- Decision: <X> h/week on content for next <Y> months, regardless of feature backlog noise

## Anti-patterns (the dignity-burners)
- Cross-posting same text to every platform unchanged (algos punish; readers notice)
- "Algorithm hacking" threads with no substance ("9 lessons I learned this week" with vapid lessons)
- Engagement-pod participation (LinkedIn detects + downranks)
- Following → unfollowing churn games
- Posting once, then ghosting 6 weeks, then posting "I'm back!"
- Repurposing without recontextualizing (Twitter thread copy-pasted as LinkedIn post = obvious)
- Buying followers (worse than zero — kills reach)
- "Newsletter" that's just product updates (it's an email, not a newsletter)
- AI-generated filler with no POV
- Selling on every single post

## Burnout / pivot triggers
- Skipped 4 weeks straight → revisit channel + cadence
- Engagement declining 3 months → pillar refresh
- Flat audience 6 months → growth loop audit
- Founder dread → wrong medium; switch

## Risk if skip
- Launch day = silence; cold paid acquisition CPI is 3–10× warm
- Product-led growth requires distribution; no audience = no PLG
- Competitors with audience launch faster; you ship features, they ship reach
- Burnout from cold launches (your 11th launch with 12 signups demoralizes more than 11 months of compounding)
- Distribution moat takes 12+ months; starting at launch = launch with no moat
